# How to build an AI strategy and roadmap

An AI strategy defines the objectives and concrete actions needed to realise an AI vision. This AI vision aims to create value within an organisation, where AI can help on two fronts.

**Products and services:** AI can be used in existing or new products. It can help enter new market segments, increase customer satisfaction and deliver a more personalised customer experience.

**Processes:** AI can optimise processes within an organisation. Complex processes in HR, Finance, logistics, and more can be streamlined, leading to higher efficiency and lower costs.

## How do you start building an AI strategy?

**Step 1 — Defining an AI vision and strategy.** This step ensures AI initiatives are aligned with business objectives.

**Step 2 — Identifying AI use cases.** In this step, AI use cases are identified, prioritised and matched to the right AI application. These applications can be in-house solutions or existing solutions available on the market.

**Step 3 — Activating and monitoring the AI success factors.** The identified AI applications need an AI-ready foundation. Without these factors, an AI application will not succeed within the organisation.

## The key success factors for AI implementation

**Data** is a crucial building block for a successful AI application. Developing and maintaining data platforms becomes increasingly important since data is the foundation of AI. Not only the data itself, but also data governance frameworks and data strategy must be developed to ensure data is managed correctly and securely within the organisation.

**Culture and organisation** are an important factor in realising an AI strategy. This involves winning trust within the organisation around the impact AI will have. Change management is an important component of this factor.

**Compliance & regulation:** AI brings many opportunities but also raises questions around privacy, data accuracy and legal requirements (cf. AI Act). A correct and proactive governance framework must be built to identify and mitigate these risks.

**Infrastructure and application architecture:** AI applications require different standards for scalability, security and maintainability. Building applications and the full SDLC cycle must be aligned with new evolutions in the development of AI applications. Spec-driven design and increased A/B testing enable faster deployment cycles and value creation for an organisation.
